About Adele

Adele Laurie Blue Adkins, known mononymously as Adele, is an English singer-songwriter. Born on May 5, 1988, in Tottenham, London, she is known for her deep, expressive contralto vocals and her eclectic mix of musical genres including soul, pop, and R&B. Adele's profound lyrics and soul-stirring music have made her one of the world's best-selling music artists with multiple accolades, including 15 Grammy Awards.

Discography

Some of Adele's most influential albums include:

  • 19 (2008) - The debut album that introduced her unique sound to the world.
  • 21 (2011) - Features hits like "Rolling in the Deep" and "Someone Like You." It won numerous awards and was a global success.
  • 25 (2015) - Known for "Hello," this album cemented Adele's status as a global superstar.
  • 30 (2021) - Her latest album, reflecting personal growth and change.
